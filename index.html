Hello and welcome! This is a website composed from the exploration team that include our members: Kavithan, Lucy, Anish and Jimmy. Our tasks was to adopt a lonely computer and explore the various hardware it had within its system. We then identified and labeled the parts and recorded our findings. Afterwards, we as a team rebuilt the computer into its original state and put all its pieces where they belonged. This website will inform you about what we learned and what pieces we found in that unused computer. We have provided a description of what that piece of hardware is and what its purpose it. Hopefully you will learn how each of the pieces interact and work in order for computers operate today.

Before

First we had to adopt a computer that was lying on the edges of the classroom just collecting dust and taking up space. With help with a screwdriver tool set we were able to open up the computer case and take a first look at what hardware pieces we had. We saw familiar pieces and some pieces that we didn't know, then we began taking apart these pieces and taking some pictures. Here is what the computer looked like before we did anything to it.

Motherboard

This piece of hardware is call the motherboard. This is the piece which keeps everything together and connected. It is like a road for other hardware to communticate to each other and send data throughout. It holds the CPU, memory chips like RAM and ROM, power connection, and other elements that may be needed to operate the computer. The silicon material used for the motherboard is ideal because its semiconductive property is superb in transfering data and power.

Power Supply

In the corner of the computer you will usually find what is called a power supply. This unit basically just distributes the needed voltages to each component of the computer.
